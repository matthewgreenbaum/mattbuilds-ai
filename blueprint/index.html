Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Campaign Blueprint

What is Campaign Blueprint?

Campaign Blueprint is an AI-powered tool that transforms your campaign ideas into executive-ready strategic blueprints. It acts as your strategic consultant, VP-level marketer, and collaborative partner — all rolled into one.

Who is this for?

B2B marketers leading integrated campaigns, whether at startups or enterprise technology companies. It's ideal for anyone who needs a structured, repeatable approach to building and pitching campaign strategies.

How long does it take to complete a blueprint?

A typical session takes 15–25 minutes. The AI guides you through six phases — Foundation, Strategy, Insight & Thesis, Planning & Requirements, Stress Test, and Decision — one question at a time, so you can move at your own pace.

Can I come back and finish later?

Yes! Your progress is automatically saved. Just select your campaign from the sidebar to pick up where you left off.

What happens at the end?

You'll receive a complete strategic blueprint with an executive summary, core insight, hypothesis, audience context, needs assessment, risk analysis, KPIs, and an advancement recommendation with leadership pitch prep. You can copy it, export to PDF, or export to Google Docs.

What are the six phases?

The conversation is organized into six phases: Foundation (campaign name, concept, company context), Strategy (audience, sales motion, business goal, KPIs & measurement), Insight & Thesis (core insight and hypothesis), Planning & Requirements (timeline, scope, content & creative assets, channels, sales enablement, teams & ops, localization), Stress Test (blind spots, strategic quality assessment), and Decision (advancement recommendation with leadership pitch prep). The AI provides a transitional summary between each phase to keep you oriented.

Will the AI make up data or metrics?

No. The AI never fabricates company priorities, metrics, or impact estimates. It will ask you for numbers or offer to help you forecast — but it won't invent them.

Can I skip questions?

The flow is designed to collect the minimum inputs needed for a rigorous blueprint. While you can't skip strategic sections, the AI won't over-probe — brief answers are perfectly fine.

Is my data stored or shared?

Your campaigns are saved locally in your browser. No campaign data is stored on any server beyond the AI conversation itself.

How It Works

A transparent look at how your blueprint gets built

Campaign Blueprint follows a structured, six-phase conversation. The AI guides you through each phase one question at a time, with transitional moments between phases to keep you oriented.

Phase 1: Foundation
1
Campaign Name

Give your campaign a working title, or skip and name it later.

2
Campaign Concept

Share the high-level elevator pitch — what's the big idea?

3
Company & Product Context

Tell us about your company, product, and how it helps your audience.

Phase 2: Strategy
4
Target Audience & Sales Motion

Define your ICP, personas, and which sales motions apply (SMB, Mid-Market, Enterprise, etc.).

5
Business Goal & Impact Type

What's the primary business objective? Pipeline, adoption, retention, positioning?

6
KPIs & Measurement

Define how you'll measure success — primary and secondary KPIs, plus key milestones and review points.

Phase 3: Insight & Thesis
7
Core Insight

Identify the specific truth about your audience or market that this campaign is built on. The AI can suggest options or you can write your own.

8
Hypothesis

Structure your campaign hypothesis: If we [action] for [persona], then [outcome] because [insight]. The AI helps with forecasting.

Phase 4: Planning & Requirements
9
Timeline

Set rough timing — expected duration and key milestones.

10
Scope & Size

T-shirt size your campaign (S/M/L/XL) based on channels, content, teams, and duration.

11
Needs Assessment

Five streamlined questions: content & creative assets, channel mix, sales enablement, teams & ops requirements, and localization.

Phase 5: Stress Test
12
Blind Spots & Assumptions

The AI proactively surfaces risks and blind spots you might have missed — this is the coaching moment.

13
Strategic Quality Assessment

The AI evaluates alignment, impact potential, and readiness — then you react and discuss.

Phase 6: Decision
14
Advancement Decision

Final recommendation: Move forward, refine, or rework. Plus leadership pitch prep tips.

Notes Jot down ideas, feedback, or anything on your mind.
